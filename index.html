And PUP's four most recent presidents

The Polytechnic University of the Philippines (PUP) is a state university located in Manila, Philippines. Established in 1904, it has grown into one of the country's largest universities, offering a wide array of undergraduate and graduate programs across various disciplines. PUP is renowned for its commitment to providing quality education, particularly in the fields of business, engineering, and information technology.



"Dr. Prudente is credited with revitalizing public education in the Philippines during his tenure. He developed PUP's physical facilities in Manila and other campuses, restructured the university's organization, and enhanced its academic and co-curricular programs."

Dr. Nemesio E. Prudente (1986–1991)

"Dr. Olonan served as PUP's first female president. During her administration, the university established its Information and Technology Center (ICTC) in 1999, which began operations in 2000. Under her leadership, PUP was recognized as a Center of Development for Excellence in Information Technology by the Commission on Higher Education in 2000."

Dr. Zenaida A. Olonan (1990s)

"Dr. Guevarra's tenure saw the creation of the web-based application system PUP iApply and the designation of PUP as Southeast Asia's first railway academy in 2007. The university also became a site for the nation's commemoration of the 110th Anniversary of the Proclamation of Philippine Independence. Notable structures built during his term include the Mabini Obelisk and the Freedom Plaza."

Dr. Dante G. Guevarra (2005–2011)

"Dr. De Guzman, a sociologist and educator, served two consecutive terms as PUP's president. Before his presidency, he was the Director of the Center for Human Development and Chairperson of the Department of Sociology and Anthropology at PUP. His leadership focused on academic excellence and institutional development. "

Dr. Emanuel C. De Guzman (2012–2020)
